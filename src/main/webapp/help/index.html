Step 0. Data Set Go to Index

Download your sample data sets (Right Click -> Save As...):
  • DNA Microarray
    • Gutierrez et al. [TRAIN]
    • Gutierrez et al. [DIAGNOSTIC]
    • Bullinger et al. [TRAIN]
    • Bullinger et al. [DIAGNOSTIC]
    • Valk et al. [TRAIN]
    • Valk et al. [DIAGNOSTIC]
  • RNA-Seq
    • TCGA Batches 93 & 96 [TRAIN]
    • TCGA Batches 103 & 109 [DIAGNOSTIC]

DNA microarray datasets were taken from the following work:

Gene expression profile reveals deregulation of genes with relevant functions in the different subclasses of acute myeloid leukemia
Gutiérrez N.C., López-Pérez R., Hernández J.M., Isidro I., González B., Delgado M., Fermiñán E., García J.L., Vázquez L., González M. et al.
Leukemia 2005, 19(3):402-409

Use of gene-expression profiling to identify prognostic subclasses in adult acute myeloid leukemia
Bullinger, L., Döhner, K., Bair, E., Fröhling, S., Schlenk, R. F., Tibshirani, R., et al.
The New England Journal of Medicine 2004, 350(16):1506-1516

Prognostically useful gene-expression profiles in acute myeloid leukemia
Valk, P. J., Verhaak, R. G., Beijen, M. A., Erpelinck, C. A., van Waalwijk, Barjesteh., van Doorn-Khosrovani
The New England Journal of Medicine 2004, 350(16):1617-1628

These datasets were preprocessed and normalized using RMA technique.

RNA-Seq datasets were downloaded from The Cancer Genome Atlas.

Please, note that although diagnostic datasets usually do not include the class of the samples, the files listed above do. We decided to include the classes in these files to facilitate the evaluation of geneCommittee. Nevertheless, geneCommittee will completely ignore the assigned classes in the diagnostic step.
